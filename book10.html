Why I’m no Longer Talking to White People about Race

By Reni Eddo-Lodgeg

Scroll Down

This book was originally published in 2017, but in June 2020 Eddo-Lodge became the first Black British writer to top the the UK book charts.

This book is part auto-biography, part evidence based narrative which breaks down race relations in Britain and the issues that arise from this fractious relationship.


The origins of the book lie in a 2014 article from Eddo-Lodge, when proclaimed that she would no longer engage with the vast majority of white people on the topic of race, because they “refuse to accept the legitimacy of structural racism and its symptoms.”
The book talks about many of the shortcomings of current society in regards to dealing with race. These include but are not limited to:

  • Race and Class and how they are not separate
  • The ludicrous right wing rhetoric of the “white working class”
  • Racism in feminism and intersectionality
  • How the social and economic elite use racism to divert attention away from their exploitation
  • The deafness of white people on race issues
  • Structural Racism
  • The history of racism in Britain
  • British colonialism
  • White Privilege and how best to use it
  • Fear of a BME world, where white people are a minority
  • Racist groups like the BNP, National Front and the EDL
  • How the system is created to use racism to its benefit
  • How you can help fight racism
  • The longevity of racism

The book is written beautifully and outlines theses issues concisely, yet in great detail. This is a must-read if you want to become actively anti-racist, or if you are currently campaigning for fundamental change in our backwards society.
