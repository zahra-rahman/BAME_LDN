Do Right For Everyone's Rights

By Tiana Cheung

Scroll Down

We are living in a world where there are two deadly pandemics

However, COVID-19 is addressed and taken seriously by the older generation whereas the other is powered by the younger generations actions and dedication to making Black, Asian, Ethnic minority. (BAME) voices heard. Now, this statement is merely a general outline of the picture our society has painted for us. There are people from the older generation who are keen for change too, but this movement would not have caused an uproar without the advanced technology we have in the 21st Century, as well as, the voices of the youth. The BAME/BLM movement is needless to say long overdue. Some may deem that protesting is a way out for people who are 'bored' of the lockdown COVID-19. Which may be the case for some, but it is wrong to put to shame the actions that people are taking for BAME's voices to be heard.


Can't you see, the systemic racism mindset is pouring through? Whenever racism comes to play, our society attempts to create diversions and to be dismissive of what is right in front of them.

I am so proud and grateful that the community of Latymer support this change. Whether it be through educating themselves, using their social media platforms, finding ways to communicate with family members who may not understand the movement, and even creating an informative website like this one on their own accord. Hopefully, we can create a road for the future generation to grow up in an environment where racism is eradicated. As well as gaining confidence in ourselves to know that racism should not be permitted. It is okay to feel scared, but we are not an oversensitive 'snowflake' generation, we are the generation that finally stands up for right instead of being submissive to society.
I understand and relate to anyone who feels degraded and disappointed when their families do not understand and think it is better to stay silent. But for those who feel in such a way, know that your voice is yours, your life is yours if you know that what you want to do is the RIGHT thing, then pick yourself up from that disappointment and push yourself to pursue using your voice in helping others.

'This is not a moment, this is a movement.'
'The time is always right to do what is right.'
- Martin Luther King
